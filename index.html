About Me

Who Am I?

Hi I'm Cynthia I enjoy puzzles figuring out how things work. It's why I love programming. I want to move the player or the AI to patrol interestingly, but how do I do that? I enjoy solving the problems and the questions that come with programming. Programming is a forever puzzle that I will continue trying to solve.

I started my journey at UCF to study game design. I decided on UCF because of a conference on women in video games hosted by UCF in January of 2018. It was the first time I felt like I found what I want to do: I found a passion that I hadn’t felt for a while. I’ve always enjoyed playing video games, but the thought of making them had never occurred to me. Playing Legend of Zelda on the N64 for hours as a kid, playing Mario on the NES with my friends, and even playing Halo with my brother are great memories from my childhood.

Fox in the Woods (Unity C#)

A crafting base survival casual gameplay.

My contributions:
Gameplay Mechanics: I programmed the player and the companion bird that slingshots around.
AI programming: Using the A* algorithm, I programmed the enemies to attack, chase, and run away from the player

Piriate Defender (Unity C#)

A pirate ship defense game.

This was a solo game project. I used the breadth-first search algorithm to move the enemy ships towards the player pirate ship.

A game about being a cat in debt to the cat mob.

My contributions:
Gameplay Mechanics: I programmed the player
UI programming: I programmed all the UI that is in the game.
AI Programming: I programmed the AI to follow a laser pointer to move them away from the player.
